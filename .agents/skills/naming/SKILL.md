# Naming Skill: High-Impact Presentation Titles

This skill defines the process for creating irresistible and strategic names for presentations, products, and themes. It moves beyond "vibes" to use marketing psychology and linguistic science.

## 🎯 The MAGIC Formula
Every title should aim to include as many of these elements as possible:

*   **M - Magnet (The "Reason Why"):** Give the audience a reason to pay attention (e.g., "Secret," "New," "Proven").
*   **A - Avatar (Target Audience):** Be specific about who this is for (e.g., "For E-commerce Founders").
*   **G - Goal (The Ultimate Benefit):** Name the dream outcome, not just the topic. (e.g., "Anti-Gravity Sales" instead of "Sales Improvement").
*   **I - Interval (Timeframe):** How long until they see results? (e.g., "24-Hour," "7-Day").
*   **C - Container (The Format):** What is this? (e.g., "Blueprint," "Masterclass," "Manifesto").

## 🧠 Linguistic Science (Stickiness)
Use these techniques to make titles more memorable and "fluent" for the brain:

*   **CVCV Pattern:** Consonant-Vowel-Consonant-Vowel (e.g., "Sony," "Lulu," "Meta"). It’s easy to process.
*   **Power Letters:**
    *   **P, K, B:** Convey reliability and strength.
    *   **Z, X:** Convey speed and innovation.
*   **Stickiness Factors:** Use **Alliteration** (e.g., "Buy or Bounce") or **Rhyming** to increase memorability.

## 🚫 The "Anti-Safe" Strategy
*   **Reject Literal Descriptions:** Avoid "The E-commerce Presentation." Instead, use something that creates energy like "Conversion Chaos."
*   **Avoid the "Sea of Sameness":** If every competitor uses "Growth," you use "Explosion" or "Launchpad."
*   **Embrace Polarization:** If a name feels "edgy" or slightly uncomfortable, it likely has the energy needed to grab attention.

## 🛠 Execution Steps
1.  **Map the Category:** Identify common titles in the industry and intentionally move away from them.
2.  **Generate 20+ Options:** Don't settle for the first "good" idea.
3.  **Context Test:** Imagine the title on a bold, oversized slide. Does it stop the scroll?
4.  **BoringClicks Filter:** Apply high contrast. Black on White, with Electric Yellow highlights on the "Goal" or "Magnet" words.
